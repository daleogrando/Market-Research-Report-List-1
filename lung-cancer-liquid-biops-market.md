<p><p>The lung cancer liquid biopsy market primarily focuses on three types: exosomes, circulating tumor cells (CTCs), and circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA). Exosomes are small vesicles that carry RNA and proteins from tumor cells, providing insights into tumor biology. CTCs are whole cancer cells found in the bloodstream, offering direct evidence of cancer presence. ctDNA consists of fragmented DNA released from tumors into circulation, allowing for genetic profiling and mutation detection, crucial for personalized treatment strategies. Together, they enhance lung cancer diagnosis and monitoring.</p></p>
