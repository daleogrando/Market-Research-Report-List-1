<p><p>The contraceptive drug market is primarily categorized into three main types: oral contraceptives, contraceptive injectables, and topical contraceptive patches. Oral contraceptives, commonly known as the pill, are taken daily to prevent ovulation. Contraceptive injectables are administered via injection every few months, offering longer-term protection. Topical contraceptive patches are applied to the skin and release hormones to prevent pregnancy for a week at a time. Each type offers unique advantages in terms of convenience, efficacy, and user compliance.</p></p>
