<p><p>Mechanical vacuum pumps come in various types, each suited for specific applications. Piston pumps utilize reciprocating motion for high vacuum; diaphragm pumps offer oil-free operation with chemical resistance. Scroll pumps provide quiet and efficient operation. Roots pumps excel in high throughput applications, while screw pumps are ideal for low-pressure tasks. Claw pumps are compact and versatile. Rotary vane pumps are known for their reliability in intermediate vacuum ranges, and turbomolecular pumps achieve ultra-high vacuum, serving scientific and industrial needs. Each type addresses unique vacuum requirements.</p></p>
