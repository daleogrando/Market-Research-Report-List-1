<p><p>The Automotive Polyamide Resin (PA) market is segmented into two main types: reactive and nonreactive. Reactive polyamide resins are designed to undergo chemical changes during curing, enhancing bonding and durability in automotive applications. These are commonly used in high-performance parts requiring superior thermal and mechanical properties. Nonreactive polyamide resins, on the other hand, remain stable and do not undergo significant changes when processed. They are typically employed in applications where flexibility and ease of processing are prioritized.</p></p>
