<p><p>Multiphoton Laser Scanning Microscopy (MPLSM) is classified into two primary types based on the microscope configuration: upright and inverted. The upright microscope allows for imaging samples from above, ideal for biological tissues and thick specimens, enabling better illumination and access. In contrast, the inverted microscope provides a bottom-up approach, beneficial for viewing living cells in culture dishes. Both types leverage advanced multiphoton techniques for enhanced imaging depth and reduced photodamage, catering to diverse research and clinical applications.</p></p>
