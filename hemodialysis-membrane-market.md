<p><p>The hemodialysis membrane market comprises two main types: cellulose-based membranes and synthetic polymer membranes. Cellulose-based membranes, derived from natural materials, are often used for their biocompatibility and effective solute removal. In contrast, synthetic polymer membranes, made from materials like polysulfone and polyacrylonitrile, offer enhanced performance characteristics, such as improved permeability and selectivity. Both types serve critical roles in kidney dialysis treatments, catering to varying patient needs and preferences based on factors like efficiency, compatibility, and cost.</p></p>
